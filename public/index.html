About Me

Like the changing of the tides, It's hard to pinpoint exactly when I realized I needed to make a career change. After 7 years in a 100% commision Sales Representative position that challenged my mental and physical agility I finally came to a place where I realized I was no longer learning or developing myself. So I decided to make a change. After Months of applying for jobs I finally started to get interviews and then Covid-19 came in to the picture and all my plans went out the door.

That's when I decided to stop "talking" about coding and try it out. So at the onset of Covid-19, I realized that there are a lot of aspects of coding that fit who I am and what I enjoy. Namely, I love learning because I love games, and I love games because I love learning. I love studying an obstacle/developing a strategy, => applying the strategy/learning from what worked and what didn’t work => developing a strategy => applying the strategy etc.. I took the 101 course and was really interested so I began freecodecamp.org’s responsive web design certificate(300 hr) and after earning that, I realized that I really enjoyed the sleuthing aspect of trying to find the bug that’s preventing my desired outcome, and the solution to a problem. So I started their javascript certificate (300 hr) and really enjoyed the similarity of algorithms to puzzles, and the creative opportunity to ask myself how I want to approach solving this puzzle knowing that there are many many ways to arrive at the same solution. After several Months of hard work I tested in to the Intermediate Software Development Class at Codefellows with a scheduled graduation date of Oct 12th. I am a hard-working, articulate, and considerate indiviual and I look forward applying myself to the challenges ahead.
